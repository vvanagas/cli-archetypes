  - id: OI-02
    title: Outcome-phrased triggers should become structural predicates
    severity: medium
    touches: server-archetype-control-map.txt
    where: >
      query_read_model.recoverable_read_errors_need_branching,
      http_command.client_needs_side_effect_free_validation,
      <several>.correctness_depends_on_real_SQL_or_constraints,
      (bff_aggregator.partial_failure_policy now holds only C43, same smell)
    finding: >
      These triggers name the OUTCOME or REQUIREMENT ("needs branching",
      "client needs", "policy") rather than a code-observable condition. They
      are judgment-grounded by construction and are the conditionals most
      likely to flip run-to-run. Reword to predicates evaluable from code shape,
      e.g.: recoverable_read_errors_need_branching -> read_has_2plus_recoverable_outcomes;
      client_needs_side_effect_free_validation -> endpoint_exposes_dryrun_flag;
      correctness_depends_on_real_SQL_or_constraints -> touches_locks_constraints_or_views;
      partial_failure_policy -> (C43) downstream_timeout_fallback_required.
    benefit: improves run-to-run consistency AND makes triggers lintable. Pairs
      with OI-08 (structural-vs-judgment tagging).

  - id: OI-17
    title: Coverage gap — per-key ordering invariant under consumer parallelism
    severity: low
    touches: server-controls.txt (candidate), server-archetype-control-map.txt
    finding: >
      Queues decompose into five axes (bound, overflow policy, order,
      producer-consumer shape, durability). The catalog deliberately legislates
      only bound + policy (C29) — correct per the selection rule, since the
      other axes yield trade-offs, not checkable obligations — and durability
      routes to C07-ceiling/C11, priority to C53, PC-shape to mechanism-plane
      default posture. The exposed gap: ordering AS AN INVARIANT. When
      correctness depends on per-key FIFO (events for aggregate X must apply in
      order) while competing consumers or parallel processing can reorder,
      nothing fires. C49 covers cursor gaps, CL05 covers client-side
      out-of-order display; "ordering guarantee violated by consumer
      parallelism" has no owner. Recurring production shape: Kafka
      partition-key mistakes, parallel webhook processing, chunk application
      races (the C34/C10 aggregate case touches it but only inside one row's
      transaction, not across the consumer topology).
    check_shape: >
      Exists — one of: partition/serialize by key (single consumer per key),
      key-affine dispatch, or a re-sequencing buffer (which then needs
      C34-style expiry for the gap that never arrives). Proof candidate:
      out_of_order_delivery_for_same_key_applies_in_order.
    blocked_on: >
      recurrence — zero real slices so far; surfaced analytically (2026-07-02,
      C29 axis-coverage audit during the state-machine/actor CLI worked
      example), not empirically. Per the promotion rule this stays a ledger
      line until seen twice in real slices. Do not pre-build.
